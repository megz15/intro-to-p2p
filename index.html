What is P2P?

Peer to Peer networking, or P2P, is a decentralized network architecture where each peer, or node, has equal privilege, and can provide and consume resources to and from other peers in the network.

What is a peer?

Peers are the participants of the network. Each peer serves as both a client and a server, and can share data and resources with other peers in the network.

Diff from traditional network arch

In a Client-Server model, data and resources are stored on a central server which is accessed by clients.

In contrast, P2P eliminates the need for a central server and relies on direct peer connections.
